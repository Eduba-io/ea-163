01 / The read

More surface area than headcount.

That shape is the shape the Eduba team knows well. Product companies with a technical founder, a small senior engineering bench, and more surface area than headcount.

The compounding tax is context. Every new contributor reconstructs decisions that are already in someone's head. Every external artifact drifts a little from what the code actually does. Every time a competitor ships a story, the team has to re-align the narrative.

Arista. Aviz. Nexthop. Upscale. Spectrum-X itself. The category framing is still being negotiated in public, and a lean team pays the tax of holding that line.

02 / The frame

60 / 30 / 10.

60%

Traditional code and DB

Already written. Already yours. Fabric in Go, dataplane in Rust, fabricator, docs. Postgres where Postgres does the job faster and cheaper, with better guarantees.

30%

Rule-based logic

SONiC policies. Fabric config. The Kubernetes CRDs that are the product. Deterministic, testable, reviewable. No LLM belongs here.

10%

Where a language model actually helps

Most teams at your stage put LLMs in all three layers and pay for it. The interesting question is where the 10% sits inside Hedgehog specifically.

03 / The closest client

Feeld. CTO-led. Product company. Same shape.

Feeld CTO-led. A scoped sprint covering workshop, advisory, and an Organizational Context Architecture. The deliverable was not a slide deck. It was a context layer the team could keep using after the engagement closed.

The Hedgehog shape maps cleanly. Technical-founder-led. Product-company posture. Multiple surfaces to coordinate across a senior engineering bench. Scoped, not six months. Methodology transfer, not a vendor relationship.

Folder structure as agent architecture.
